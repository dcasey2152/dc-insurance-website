Private Market Coverage

Private PPO Health Insurance in Tennessee

What it is, who it's built for, and how it compares to ACA marketplace plans — explained plainly.

What Is a Private PPO Health Insurance Plan?

A private market PPO plan is health insurance purchased directly from an insurance carrier — outside the ACA marketplace, outside your employer, on an individual basis. Unlike ACA plans, these are medically underwritten: the carrier reviews your health history before issuing coverage and pricing your policy.

That one difference — medical underwriting — changes the math significantly for people in good health.

Who Private PPO Plans Are Built For

Private market PPO plans tend to work best for people who are:

  • In good to excellent health with no significant pre-existing conditions
  • Self-employed or 1099 workers earning above the ACA subsidy range
  • Small business owners who need individual coverage
  • High-income households looking for maximum flexibility and lower cost-sharing
  • Frequent travelers who need nationwide network access without referrals

If you have significant pre-existing conditions that would result in coverage exclusions or high rate-ups, the ACA marketplace is typically the better fit — that's what it was designed for. We'll give you an honest read on which lane makes sense for your situation.

How Private PPO Plans Compare to ACA Plans

The biggest practical differences come down to four things:

Network Flexibility

Most private PPO plans offer nationwide coverage with no referral requirements. ACA marketplace plans — particularly in Tennessee — are often HMO or EPO structures with regional networks. If you have doctors you want to keep seeing, or you travel frequently, this matters.

Premium Pricing

ACA plans use community rating — everyone in your age bracket and area pays essentially the same premium regardless of health. Private plans price based on your individual health history. Healthy individuals often pay significantly less. Less healthy individuals may be rated up or see exclusions applied.

Cost-Sharing Structure

Private PPO plans can be structured with lower deductibles and more favorable coinsurance than comparable ACA options at the same premium level. We look at the full cost picture — premium plus expected out-of-pocket — not just the monthly payment.

Enrollment Flexibility

Unlike ACA plans, private market plans don't require a qualifying life event or open enrollment window. If you're healthy and want to switch, you can apply anytime. Explore the full ACA vs. private plan comparison on our dedicated page.
